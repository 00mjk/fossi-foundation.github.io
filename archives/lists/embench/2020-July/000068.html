Hello David,

Thank you for your submission to Arm DevSummit 2020. The quality and
quantity of submissions this year’s virtual event were at an all-time high,
making the selection process extremely competitive.

Due to the limited number of sessions for each track and the amount
submitted on similar topics, many high-quality submissions were not able to
be incorporated into the program. After much deliberation by the Technical
Program Committee, your submission was not chosen to be included in this
year’s conference program.

The following session(s) you submitted have been declined:
------------------------------



*Session Title:* Embench: A New, Free, Desperately-Needed Benchmark Suite
for Embedded CPUs
*Session Abstract: *Embedded computing suffers from old terrible benchmarks
such as CoreMark (2009) and Dhrystone (1984). A group from academia and
industry developed Embench, a suite of 20 real programs. We show Embench’s
value by answering a wide range of questions about the most popular
embedded architectures with respect to performance, code size, instruction
features, libraries, compilers, optimizations, ...



*Speaker Information: * Individual or Co-Speaker, Session Submitter, David
Patterson, Professor, UC Berkeley
------------------------------
